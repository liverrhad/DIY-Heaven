Cookings pretty easy, but you need some help

     If you're new to dieting and you look into your fridge, there's a big chance that you're not going to have anything too healthy. So we decided to help you out and show you some recipes and different websites to help you get started. I would also recommend apps like Lifesum or MyFitnessPal to help get you started. Also if you feel like working out, but you want to help out the world, try Chairty Miles which donates money to charity as you run/bike/walk. Besides all of that, let's dive into some good recipes.

Breakfast

Salmon Scrambled Eggs


Original Link: http://www.foodnetwork.com/recipes/rachael-ray/scrambled-eggs-with-smoked-salmon-recipe.html

     These eggs are the king of all breakfast foods. They are great for getting you going in the morning and keeping you going all day. Not only are they jacked with protein but they are only 235 calories per serving. Also making these eggs only takes about 15-20 minutes at the most. Over all this is the go to breakfast to get you pumped up for the day.

Lunch

BLATs (Bacon-Lettuce-Avocado-Tomato Sandwiches)


Original Link: http://www.eatingwell.com/recipes/blats_bacon_lettuce_avocado_tomato_sandwiches.html

     Now these sandwiches are the bomb diggity! They are everything you love about a BLT but also has all of the health (and taste) benefits of avocados and is only about 350 calories per serving. This is very fast especially if you already have everything prepped and ready to go. This is my number one choice when it comes to BLT’s or any sandwich for that matter.

Dinner

Pork Cutlets with Butternut Squash, Apple, and Cranberry Sauté


Original Link: http://www.myrecipes.com/recipe/pork-cutlets-butternut-squash

     This is one of my personal favorites, at least, as far as dinner goes. The apple and cranberries in the saute give the pork as sweet and savory flavor and makes this dish very unique. I always prepare an additional side to go with this such as garlic roasted broccoli.
